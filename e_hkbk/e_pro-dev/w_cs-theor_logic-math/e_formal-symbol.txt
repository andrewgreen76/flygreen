
===================================================================================
FORMAL SYMBOL 
===================================================================================

 . a character or string as a recognized way of communicating a (mathematical or
   logical) object or concept 


Formal symbols in prepositional logic :

Propositional Variables: P,Q,R, ... (representing statements)
Negation: ¬ (not)
Conjunction: ∧ (and)
Disjunction: ∨ (or)
Implication: → (implies)
Biconditional: ↔ (if and only if)
Truth: ⊤ (true)
Falsity: ⊥ (false)


Formal symbols in predicate logic :

Predicate Variables: P(x),Q(x,y),…P(x),Q(x,y),… (representing properties or relations)
Quantifiers:

    Universal Quantifier: ∀∀ (for all)
    Existential Quantifier: ∃∃ (there exists)

Logical Connectives:

    Negation: ¬¬ (not)
    Conjunction: ∧∧ (and)
    Disjunction: ∨∨ (or)
    Implication: →→ (implies)
    Biconditional: ↔↔ (if and only if)

Variables: x,y,z,…x,y,z,… (representing individual elements)
Constants: a,b,c,…a,b,c,… (specific elements in the domain)
Function Symbols: f(x),g(x,y),…f(x),g(x,y),… (representing functions)
