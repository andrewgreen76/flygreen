for programming and scripting. There are languages for :

========================================================================================
UNSTRUCTURED VS. STRUCTURED : 
========================================================================================

Unstructured :
  |
  . characteristics :
  | . goto statements
  |
  . languages :
  | . assembly
  | . BASIC 
  | . Fortran 
  |
Structured :
 . program control (flow) structures : 
   . sequence 
   . condition 
   . iteration 
   . blocks
   . subroutines 

========================================================================================
IMPERATIVE VS. DECLARATIVE : 
========================================================================================

Imperative :
 . explicitly specified statements to achieve something
 . (but this does NOT mean programming over scripting) 
 . strongly tied to procedural programming
 . FORTRAN , COBOL , ALGOL , BASIC , C / C++ , Python , Java 

Declarative :
 . a command or statement is given to get something done ; the processing utility will
   understand/translate this into the steps implied to achieve the goal
 . (but this does NOT mean scripting over programming) 
 . database query languages (e.g., SQL, XQuery)
 . regular expressions
 . logic programming 
 . strongly tied to functional programming
 . configuration management
 . algebraic modeling systems
 . Lisp , ML , Prolog , Datalog , ASP 

========================================================================================
LOGIC PROGRAMMING : 
========================================================================================

 . strongly tied to functional programming

 . Datalog
 . answer set programming (ASP) 
 . Haskell

========================================================================================
PROCEDURAL VS. FUNCTIONAL : 
========================================================================================

Procedural :
  |
  . characteristics :
  | . mutable state
  |
  . languages : 
  | . C
  | . Pascal
  | . COBOL
  | . BASIC
  |
Functional :
  |
  . characteristics :
  | . evaluation of mathematical expressions 
  | . independent functions ; same input -> same output
  |
  | . immutable state
  | . immutable data
  | . first-class (higher-order) functions
  |
  . languages : 
    . Haskell
    . Lisp
    . Scheme
    . Clojure
    . Erlang
