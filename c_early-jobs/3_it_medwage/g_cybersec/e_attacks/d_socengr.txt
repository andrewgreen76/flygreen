
===================================================================================
SOCIAL ENGINEERING - BY TECHNICAL AND IN-PERSON MEANS
===================================================================================

 . hacking humans


Phishing Attacks:
 . Email attack:
    . "Your account has been compromised" + a sketchy link to a fake duplicate of a real website
    . targets an arbitrary user
 . Spear fishing:
    . more deceptive as it can get retailored (personalized) to sound more trustworthy 
    . can target an individual or even a group
 . "JavaScript can be employed to create convincing phishing websites that trick users into revealing sensitive
    information such as login credentials, personal details, or financial information."


(Email) spoofing:
 . a malicious email sent from a real address ("masquerading") but featuring a link to a malicious IP address


Baiting:
 . hacking humans directly instead of using computers


Tailgating:
 . entering the premises by following an employee inside, oftentimes impersonating a third-party employee


Shoulder Surfing:
 . An in-person attack where an attacker observes a victim entering sensitive information, such as passwords,
   to steal credentials.

Vishing:
 . Voice phishing, where attackers use phone calls to impersonate legitimate organizations to extract personal
   information from victims.
 . using VoIP


Whaling:
 . targeting a person with a great deal of fame, power, and/or wealth
 . more time is spent on tailoring the bait correctly


Impersonation:
 . impersonating IT Support to get an employee of an organization fix a credentials issue that isn't there, thereby
   redirecting the employee to a website where the new credentials would be stored and infiltrating the company's
   IT infrastructure


Dumpster diving: literally.
